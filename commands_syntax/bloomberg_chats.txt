02:49:29 BLOOMBERG HELP DESK : You can get both dates as-of any past date, but they come from different instruments, so you cannot use SGD1M Curncy for the option-expiry pull.
Use SGD1M Curncy for the forward settlement date:
=BDP("SGD1M Curncy", "SETTLE_DT", "REFERENCE_DATE", TEXT(A1, "YYYYMMDD"))

Use the FX pair surface ticker for the option expiry date for a given tenor:
=BDP("USDSGD BGN Curncy", "VOL_SURF_EXPIRY_OVR", "VOL_SURF_MTY_OVR=1M", "REFERENCE_DATE", TEXT(A1, "YYYYMMDD"))

SGD1M Curncy is an FX forward tenor ticker, and the option-expiry workflow uses the volatility surface fields on the FX pair surface ticker, not the forward tenor ticker.

In Bloomberg OVDV <GO> FX volatility surfaces, the delta convention is pair-dependent and follows the surface’s contributor or market convention, which you can view in OVDV <GO> under "Settings" > "Conventions". The key items are "Delta Style" and "Delta Premium".
For the Bloomberg BGN FX surface specifically:
- Spot vs forward delta: "Delta Style" is spot delta for maturities below 1Y and forward delta for maturities at and beyond 1Y. Confirm the exact threshold for the pair in "Delta Style".
* Premium-adjusted vs unadjusted: "Delta Premium" is premium-included for most currency pairs, and premium-excluded for EURUSD, GBPUSD, AUDUSD, NZDUSD, and precious metals.
