##########################
Rates:
##########################
IRSB <GO> is the core monitor for a country’s real-time, intraday interest rate swap yields and spreads, with historical context. 
        Use the country/region drop-down and the tabs to switch between swap types, then hover or right-click a tenor to pull up analytics like DES <GO>.

FXTF <GO> is designed for finding the available swap tickers by region, currency, and product type. It is the fastest way to browse “Interest Rate Swaps”, 
        “Basis Swaps”, and “Swap Spreads” sets

SWPM <GO> is used to build and value swaps when you need something that is not available as a standard generic ticker, or when you need to change 
        conventions or add spreads. Use “Solver” in SWPM <GO> to solve for the par fixed rate that sets NPV to zero after you set the swap terms and floating index.

BTMM <GO> provides the rates “dashboard” for a country, including money-market benchmarks and related rate instruments, which is useful context 
        alongside IRS monitoring and curve work.

SDR <GO> and SDRV <GO> are used for swap trade activity and volume views, which can help validate where liquidity is printing when you are 
        analyzing levels and spreads.

ICVS: Swap Curve Builder

FXFM : Fx Rate Forecast Model

##########################
FX:
##########################
FXIP <GO> provides a dashboard-style view by region or country, combining FX rates, charts, and related market context such as fixed income and news. 
        It includes an FX Markets Overview tab and an FX Forwards tab for forward-rate analysis.

FXC <GO> displays a matrix of spot, forward, and fixing rates, useful as a live “at-a-glance” monitor across a basket of currencies.

WCRS <GO> ranks and compares currencies across metrics such as returns, interest rates, volatility, carry trade, CDS, and PPP. 
        It supports both visual and table-style comparisons, and you can set a base currency and basket, including custom baskets.

FRD <GO> shows market-consensus FX forward points and outright forwards across standard tenors and custom dates, and it is commonly used to 
        view the forward curve for a pair.

FXFA <GO> focuses on FX carry and curve-style analytics via implied yields and FX swap relationships between spot, forwards, and interest rates.

FXFC : Fx Forecasts
